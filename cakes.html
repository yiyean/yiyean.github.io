Decadent Chocolate Delight Cake

This luscious chocolate cake is an indulgent treat perfect for any occasion. It's luxuriously rich, moist, and bursting with irresistible chocolate flavor!

Ingredients

  • 90g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 112g vegetable oil
  • 210g all-purpose flour
  • 237g freshly brewed coffee
  • 240g buttermilk, room temperature
  • 400g granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature

Instructions

  1. Sift the flour, sugar, cocoa, baking soda, baking powder, and salt into the bowl and mix until combined.
  2. In another bowl, combine the buttermilk, oil, eggs, and vanilla.
  3. With the mixer on low speed, slowly add the wet ingredients to the dry. With mixer still on low, add the coffee and stir just to combine, scraping the bottom of the bowl with a rubber spatula.
  4. Pour the batter into two 8-inch round pans and bake for 30-40 minutes at 350°F, until a cake tester comes out mostly clean (not wet).
  5. Cool in the pans for about 10 minutes, then turn them out onto a cooling rack and cool completely.

Silky Cream Cheese Delight Cake

This luscious cheesecake features a smooth and silky cream cheese filling and a delectable buttery graham cracker crumb crust. It's the perfect canvas for your favorite fruit toppings and an irresistible indulgence for any special occasion.

Ingredients

  • 75g unsalted butter, melted
  • 120g sour cream, room temperature
  • 210g graham cracker crumbs
  • 250g superfine granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons confectioners' sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 5 large eggs, room temperature
  • 4 packages (8 oz. each) cream cheese, softened

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Mix graham cracker crumbs, sugar, and butter. Make sure all graham crackers are coated in butter and that it is a coarse sand consistency.
  3. Press crust onto the bottom and 2/3 of the way up a 9-inch springform pan lined with parchment. Wrap 2 pieces of heavy-duty tin foil around the bottom and sides of the pan. Set aside.
  4. Use an electric mixer or stand mixer fitted with the whisk attachment to mix cream cheese until creamed.
  5. Beat in the sugar and scrape down the sides of the bowl.
  6. Add in the sour cream and vanilla. Blend until smooth and creamy. Scrape down sides of the bowl. With the mixer on low, add in eggs one by one until fully incorporated.
  7. Pour the filling in the crust. Carefully place the cheesecake into the pan and then place both in the oven. Fill with halfway up the side of the springform pan with water.
  8. Bake until the top of the cheesecake turns golden, about 60-70 minutes. Remove from oven. Let the cheesecake cool on a wire rack.
  9. Combine sour cream and sugar, spread over cheesecake. Cover and refrigerate at least 4 hours.

Luscious Lemon Seismic Cake

The Luscious Lemon Seismic Cake is a decadent dessert that begins with a layer of shredded coconut and crunchy macadamia nuts. This is followed by a rich and tangy lemon cake with a luscious cream cheese swirl, all topped off with a sprinkling of white chocolate chips and baked to perfection. Every bite of this dessert is sure to send seismic waves of delight through your taste buds!

Ingredients

  • 57g chopped macadamia nuts
  • 80g sweetened shredded coconut
  • 113g unsalted butter, melted
  • 168g white chocolate chips
  • 312.5g confectioners' sugar
  • ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 package (8 ounces) cream cheese, softened
  • 1 box (15.25 ounces) lemon cake mix, mixed according to package directions

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F and grease a 9×13-inch pan with cooking spray.
  2. Prepare the cake batter according to package directions.
  3. Sprinkle the coconut and macadamia nuts onto the bottom of the prepared pan.
  4. Spread your cake batter over the top. Set aside.
  5. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, combine the cream cheese, butter, confectioners' sugar, vanilla, and salt. Beat with a mixer until combined. Dollop the mixture over the cake and use a knife to marble.
  6. Sprinkle white chocolate chips on top and bake for about 55-60 minutes, or until the center is set.

Decadent Red Velvet Cake

This indulgent red velvet cake is a crowd-pleaser. The rich and moist cake is filled with velvety smooth cream cheese frosting and finished off with a sprinkling of dark chocolate shavings for a deliciously satisfying and luxurious dessert.

Ingredients

  • 39g unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 113g hot water
  • 113g unsalted butter, room temperature
  • 232g mayonnaise, room temperature
  • 245g buttermilk, room temperature
  • 250g all-purpose flour
  • 400g granulated sugar
  • 500g confectioners' sugar
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon white distilled vinegar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 1 ounce red food coloring, more or less depending on how deep you want the color
  • 8 ounces cream cheese, room temperature

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 325°F. Spray 2, 9-inch round cake pans with nonstick cooking spray and line each with parchment paper. Set aside.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking soda, baking powder, cocoa powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the mayonnaise, eggs, buttermilk, vanilla, red food coloring, hot water, and vinegar.
  4. Slowly add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, mixing until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter equally between the prepared pans. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out mostly clean. A few crumbs are what you want for a MOIST cake!
  6. Allow the cakes to cool for 10-15 minutes in the pans. Then, turn the cakes out onto a rack to cool completely. After the cakes have cooled completely, make the frosting.
  7. To a large bowl, add the butter, cream cheese, and vanilla. Using a handheld mixer on medium speed, blend until the ingredients are fully incorporated and smooth.
  8. Add confectioners' sugar, one cup at a time, until the frosting is light and creamy.
  9. Cover the cooled cake in a thin layer of frosting, putting it between the layers and around the outside of the cake. Serve.
